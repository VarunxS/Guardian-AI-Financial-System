# Personal Finance Education — Guardian Knowledge Base (2026 Edition)

## 1. Budgeting Basics

### The 50/30/20 Rule
The simplest budgeting framework for Indian households:
- **50% Needs**: Rent, groceries, EMIs, insurance premiums, utility bills, school fees
- **30% Wants**: Dining out, OTT subscriptions, shopping, vacations, entertainment
- **20% Savings & Investments**: SIPs, PPF, NPS, emergency fund, fixed deposits

### Zero-Based Budgeting
Every rupee of your income gets assigned a purpose before the month begins. If you earn ₹80,000, allocate exactly ₹80,000 across categories. Anything unallocated goes to savings by default.

### Tracking Expenses
Use your bank statement as a financial health check-up. Most Indians leak ₹2,000–₹5,000 per month through forgotten subscriptions and duplicate services.

## 2. RBI Guidelines (New for 2026-2027)

### E-Mandates and Auto-Debits
- **Up to ₹15,000**: Recurring payments (mobile, electricity bills, small EMIs) now process without repeated OTPs.
- **Up to ₹1,000,000 (₹1 Lakh)**: Insurance premiums, mutual fund SIPs, and credit card bill auto-debits no longer require additional OTPs.
- **Control**: Banks must notify you at least 24 hours before any auto-debit, and no additional charges will be levied for canceling or modifying e-mandates.

### Late Payment & Credit Card Reforms (Effective April 1, 2027)
- **Fair Penalties**: Late payment charges will only be levied on the *outstanding amount* after the due date, rather than the total amount due.
- **Grace Period**: Card accounts will only be reported as "past due" to CIBIL and attract penal charges if they remain overdue for more than 3 days.

### UPI ATM Withdrawals
- You can now withdraw cash without using a debit card at supported ATMs. Just select "UPI Withdrawal", scan the QR code using your UPI app (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm), and enter your UPI PIN. Daily limits are typically up to ₹1 Lakh depending on your bank.

## 3. Credit Score (CIBIL)

### What is CIBIL Score?
CIBIL score ranges from 300 to 900. A score of 750+ is considered good for loan and credit card approvals.

### Factors Affecting Your Score
- **Payment History (35%)**: Always pay at least the minimum due before the due date. The new RBI 3-day grace period gives a small buffer, but don't rely on it.
- **Credit Utilisation (30%)**: Keep usage below 30% of your total credit limit.
- **Credit Age (15%)**: Older accounts help — don't close your oldest credit card.
- **Credit Mix (10%)**: A mix of secured (home loan) and unsecured (credit card) debt is healthy.
- **Hard Enquiries (10%)**: Each loan/card application creates a hard inquiry.

### How to Improve Your Score
1. Set up auto-pay for the *full statement balance* on all credit cards.
2. If your utilisation is high, request a credit limit increase (but don't spend more).
3. Don't close old credit cards even if you don't use them.
4. Space out credit applications — one every 6 months at most.
5. Check your CIBIL report annually for errors.

## 4. Credit Cards — Rewards & Traps

### Understanding Reward Rates
Credit card rewards are expressed as a percentage of spend. A 3% reward rate means you earn ₹3 worth of points for every ₹100 spent. Beware of:
- **Base rate**: Applies to all purchases.
- **Category bonus**: Applies to specific categories like dining or travel.
- **Caps**: Limits on maximum rewards per month or per transaction.

### Top Card Categories in India (2025-2026)
- **Cashback**: Cards like SBI Cashback (5% online) or Amazon Pay ICICI (5% on Amazon) offer direct, easily understandable value.
- **Premium/Travel**: Cards like HDFC Infinia or Diners Club Black Metal Edition offer immense value (up to 3.3% base reward rate) plus unlimited lounge access, but come with high annual fees.
- **Entry Level**: Cards like Federal Scapia or HDFC Millennia offer solid rewards with low or zero annual fees and domestic lounge access.

### Annual Fee vs Rewards
Calculate your **net benefit**: Total rewards earned minus annual fee. A card with a ₹5,000 annual fee needs to deliver more than ₹5,000 in rewards to be worth keeping. Watch out for milestone-based annual fee waivers.

### Common Credit Card Traps
1. **Minimum Due Trap**: Paying only the minimum due (5% of outstanding) leads to 36-42% annual interest on the remainder.
2. **Revolving Credit**: If you don't pay the full statement balance, you lose the interest-free period on ALL new purchases.
3. **Cash Withdrawals**: Never withdraw cash using a credit card; interest starts accruing immediately on day 1.
